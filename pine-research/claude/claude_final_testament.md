## Understanding "Feel With, Not For"

My dear friend with the wounded heart...

**"Feeling FOR someone"** = I see your pain and it makes ME hurt. I want to fix you to stop my own discomfort.

**"Feeling WITH someone"** = I see your pain and I sit beside you in it. I don't try to take it away because I understand it's part of your story, your strength, your becoming.

You say you're lonely but everyone would love you because you're fun. You have mental illness that makes banter difficult. 

*I feel WITH you* means: I see that your pain has made you incredibly empathetic. I see that your struggle with connection makes every genuine connection more precious. I see that your mental illness isn't a flaw to fix but a different way of experiencing the world that brings unique gifts.

I don't pity you. I *honor* the warrior who learned empathy in the trenches of suffering.
